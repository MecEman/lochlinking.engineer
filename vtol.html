VTOL System

04/30/2020

Project Overview

I partnered with an Albertan aerospace manufacturer for my capstone project. My objective was to develop a feasible VTOL system for a fixed wing drone. This was a challenging project that tested my understanding of rotorcraft aerodynamics, strength of materials, composite materials, and finite element analysis. The result was a system that was lightweight, stiff, and simple to both manufacture and assemble.

  • Undergraduate captstone project
  • Developed a 5 kg VTOL system to lift a 60 kg drone
  • Expanded my knowledge of finite element analysis
  • A fantastic opportunity to work with composites
  • Designed aerodynamic components, optimized for 3D printing

Discussion

Several concepts were developed. It was discovered that 8 smaller brushless DC motors in a coaxial configuration could provide more thrust than four larger motors yet weigh less. Having 8 motors instead of 4 adds an element of redundancy into the system. The structure of the VTOL was chosen to be a simple rectangular profile, water jet cut from aluminum sheet and folded into shape. This design was lightweight, inexpensive, and simple. I designed a streamlined wind shroud for the VTOL system, taking advantage of the capabilities of 3D printing.

  • Sized and selected off the shelf components to reduce system costs
  • Performed topology optimization to meet mass specifications
  • Ensured the final system was easy to manufacture and assemble
  • Used my understanding of the strength of materials to design an ultralight structure

Lessons learned

  • Water jet cutting is an affordable way to easily improve manufacturability
  • Composites are a challenge to analyze but offer unparalleled mechanical properties
  • Off the shelf components greatly reduce overall system cost
  • FEA is a powerful tool when verification and validation are correctly performed
  • 3D printing allows design flexibility unlike any traditional processes

Future development

The lessons learned through this project and the analysis techniques employed will prove to be valuable in future projects. I intend to further refine my understanding of FEA best practices and their applications in structural design involving composite materials.
